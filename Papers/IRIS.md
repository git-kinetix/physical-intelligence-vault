# Transformers are Sample-Efficient World Models

## Summary

IRIS (Imagination with auto-Regression over an Inner Speech) introduces a data-efficient reinforcement learning agent that learns within a world model composed of a discrete autoencoder and an autoregressive transformer. Rather than using the recurrent state-space models (RSSM) employed by the [[DreamerV1|Dreamer]] series, IRIS discretizes visual observations into tokens using a VQ-VAE-style autoencoder and then models environment dynamics as a sequence prediction problem using a GPT-like transformer.

On the [[Atari 2600 Games|Atari 100K]] benchmark -- where agents must learn from only 100,000 environment interactions (equivalent to approximately two hours of gameplay) -- IRIS achieves a mean human-normalized score of 1.046, setting a new state of the art for methods without lookahead search. The agent outperforms humans on 10 out of 26 games, demonstrating that transformers can serve as highly sample-efficient world models when combined with discrete tokenization of observations.

IRIS was published at ICLR 2023 as a notable top-5% paper, and its architecture directly influenced subsequent work including [[DIAMOND]] (which replaced discrete tokens with diffusion) and DART. The approach demonstrated that the auto-regressive transformer paradigm from language modeling could be effectively transferred to world modeling for reinforcement learning.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a world model architecture combining a discrete autoencoder (VQ-VAE) with an autoregressive transformer for environment dynamics modeling
- Achieves state-of-the-art mean human-normalized score of 1.046 on [[Atari 2600 Games|Atari 100K]] without lookahead search
- Outperforms humans on 10 out of 26 Atari games with only 2 hours of gameplay experience
- Demonstrates that transformer-based sequence modeling is a viable and powerful alternative to RSSM-based world models ([[DreamerV2]]/V3)
- Provides evidence that discrete tokenization of visual observations enables effective world modeling

## Architecture / Method

IRIS consists of two main components:

**1. Discrete Autoencoder (Tokenizer):**
- A VQ-VAE-style encoder-decoder that maps 64x64 RGB observations to a grid of discrete tokens
- Each observation is compressed into a sequence of discrete latent codes from a learned codebook
- The autoencoder is trained separately to reconstruct observations from discrete tokens
- This creates a "vocabulary" of visual tokens analogous to word tokens in language

**2. Autoregressive Transformer (World Model):**
- A GPT-like causal transformer that models the dynamics of the environment
- Input sequence: interleaved observation tokens and action tokens
- The transformer predicts the next observation tokens given the history of past observation-action pairs
- Trained with standard cross-entropy loss over the discrete token vocabulary
- Generates future trajectories by autoregressively sampling tokens

**Training Pipeline:**
1. Collect real environment interactions using the current policy
2. Train the discrete autoencoder on collected observations
3. Train the autoregressive transformer on tokenized sequences
4. Train the policy (actor-critic) within imagined trajectories generated by the transformer world model
5. Repeat

**Policy Training:** An actor-critic algorithm is trained entirely within imagined trajectories generated by the transformer world model, similar in spirit to [[DreamerV1|Dreamer]] but using transformer-generated rollouts instead of RSSM-generated ones.

## Results

### Table 1: [[Atari 2600 Games|Atari 100K]] Benchmark (Mean and Median Human-Normalized Scores)

| Method | Type | Mean [[Human Normalized Mean]] | Median [[Human Normalized Mean]] | Superhuman Games |
|--------|------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **IRIS** | World Model | **1.046** | 0.45 | **10/26** |
| [[DreamerV2]] | World Model | 0.74 | 0.28 | 5/26 |
| [[DreamerV3]] | World Model | 1.12 | 0.49 | -- |
| SPR | Model-Free | 0.70 | 0.42 | -- |
| EfficientZero | World Model + Search | 1.91 | 1.09 | 17/26 |
| SimPLe | World Model | 0.44 | 0.14 | 2/26 |
| Random | -- | 0.00 | 0.00 | 0/26 |
| Human | -- | 1.00 | 1.00 | 26/26 |

IRIS achieves the highest mean [[Human Normalized Mean|HNS]] among methods without lookahead search, surpassing [[DreamerV2]] significantly. EfficientZero achieves higher scores but relies on Monte Carlo Tree Search.

## Metrics Used

- Human-[[Normalized Task Score|Normalized Score]] ([[Human Normalized Mean|HNS]]) -- primary metric: (agent_score - random_score) / (human_score - random_score), evaluated as mean and median across 26 Atari games
- Superhuman Games -- number of games where the agent exceeds human performance
- [[Sample Efficiency]] -- performance achieved within 100K environment interactions (2 hours of play)

## Datasets Used

- [[Atari 2600 Games|Atari 100K]] Benchmark -- 26 Atari 2600 games, each limited to 100K environment steps (400K frames with action repeat of 4)

## Related Papers

- [[DIAMOND]] -- direct successor by the same research group, replacing discrete tokens with a diffusion world model and achieving 1.46 mean [[Human Normalized Mean|HNS]]
- [[DreamerV3]] -- the primary RSSM-based world model competitor, which IRIS outperforms on mean [[Human Normalized Mean|HNS]] at the time of publication
- [[DreamerV2]] -- predecessor RSSM world model that IRIS significantly outperforms on [[Atari 2600 Games|Atari 100K]]
- [[DreamerV1]] -- original [[DreamerV1|Dreamer]] world model
- [[NVIDIA Cosmos]] -- large-scale world model using similar discrete tokenization at much larger scale
