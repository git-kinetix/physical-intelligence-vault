# CIFAR-10

## Description
CIFAR-10 is a compact image classification benchmark containing 60,000 tiny color images across 10 mutually exclusive classes (airplane, automobile, bird, cat, deer, dog, frog, horse, ship, truck). Created by Alex Krizhevsky, Vinod Nair, and Geoffrey Hinton at the University of Toronto, it remains one of the most popular datasets for rapid prototyping and evaluation of visual representation methods.

## Format
32x32 pixel RGB images stored in binary batches (Python pickle format).

## Size
60,000 images total: 50,000 training and 10,000 test images. Approximately 163 MB download size.
