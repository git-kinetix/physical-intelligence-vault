Exploration 03

Multi-Task Scaling
Behavior

TD-MPC2 demonstrates clear scaling laws for world models: a single 317M-parameter agent trained on 80 tasks across 12 embodiments shows consistently improving performance with model and data scale.

One of TD-MPC2's most striking results is the emergence of scaling laws for model-based RL. The paper trains models from 1M to 317M parameters on a shared dataset of 80 tasks and shows that performance increases smoothly with scale — a pattern previously observed in language models but rarely in RL.

The scaling is remarkably efficient: the 317M model requires only ~9x the GPU days of the 1M model while achieving 4.4x the normalized score. This sub-linear cost scaling suggests that scaling world models is a practical path to more capable RL agents.

Data Scaling

TD-MPC2 also investigates how performance scales with the number of tasks in the training dataset. The same model architecture trained on 15, 30, and 80 tasks shows different scaling behavior: larger datasets require larger models to fully exploit, but also enable higher ultimate performance.

Single-Task Specialist vs. Multi-Task Generalist

A natural question is whether multi-task training helps or hurts individual task performance. The data reveals a nuanced picture: small multi-task models underperform specialists, but as model capacity grows, the gap narrows and the generalist gains the advantage of broad knowledge transfer.

Task Embedding Space

TD-MPC2 conditions all model components on a 96-dimensional learnable task embedding (L2-normalized). These embeddings organize in meaningful ways: tasks with similar embodiments or reward structures cluster together, enabling the model to share representations efficiently across related tasks.
