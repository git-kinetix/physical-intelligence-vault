Interactive Exploration 05

Training Dynamics

Balancing prediction and regularization: how the single hyperparameter λ shapes the learned embedding space during training.

Training LeWM is a balancing act between two forces: the prediction loss wants to compress all embeddings into a small, predictable region, while SIGReg pushes them apart into a Gaussian cloud. The single hyperparameter λ controls this balance. Too little regularization and the representation collapses; too much and it becomes random noise.

The default λ = 0.09 was found to work well across all tested environments — PushT, TwoRoom, Reacher, and Cube. This robustness to a single hyperparameter is one of LeWM's key advantages over prior JEPAs that required tuning six loss weights.

At inference time, the trained predictor unrolls predictions autoregressively. Starting from a window of real observations, it predicts the next embedding, shifts the window forward, and repeats. Each prediction feeds into the next, creating a chain of latent-space forecasts that a planner uses to evaluate candidate action sequences.

Error accumulates with each autoregressive step — the predicted embeddings drift further from where real observations would land. This is why the planning horizon matters: longer horizons require more accurate predictions. LeWM's compact 192-dim embedding space helps control this drift.

Training uses AdamW with lr = 5×10−5, weight decay = 10−3, gradient clipping at 1.0, and bf16 precision for 100 epochs. The total loss is pred_loss + λ × sigreg_loss.
