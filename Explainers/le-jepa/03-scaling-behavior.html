Training Loss Across Scales

One of Le-JEPA's most remarkable properties is that the same training configuration works across wildly different model sizes. The figure below shows simulated training loss curves for Vision Transformers from ViT-S (22M params) to ViT-g (1.8B params). Click model sizes to toggle their curves.

Training loss curves for Le-JEPA at different model scales. All curves converge smoothly. No learning rate scheduling changes needed across scales.

Notice how all curves converge smoothly without any divergence or instability. Larger models achieve lower final loss, but the training dynamics are qualitatively identical. This is the signature of a well-conditioned optimization landscape.

The Instability Problem in Other Methods

Contrast Le-JEPA's smooth scaling with what happens in other self-supervised methods. DINO requires careful temperature scheduling that breaks at large scales. VICReg's covariance computation becomes unstable. I-JEPA needs EMA rate tuning per model size. The figure below shows these methods alongside Le-JEPA.

Le-JEPA DINO VICReg I-JEPA
At small scales (ViT-S), all methods train stably. Move the slider to larger models and watch how DINO, VICReg, and I-JEPA develop instabilities while Le-JEPA remains smooth.
At the ViT-g scale (1.8B parameters), DINO typically requires 3-5 restarts with different hyperparameters to achieve stable training. Le-JEPA trains stably on the first attempt with the same λ used for ViT-S.

Architecture-Agnostic Performance

Le-JEPA is not limited to Vision Transformers. The paper validates across five architecture families: ViTs, ConvNeXts, ResNets, MaxViTs, and Swin Transformers. The figure below shows performance across 50+ architectures from the timm library, all trained with identical hyperparameters.

Each bar is a different architecture trained with identical Le-JEPA settings on ImageNet-10. Filter by family to see that performance scales with model capacity regardless of architecture.

Loss Predicts Performance

A practical breakthrough in Le-JEPA is that the training loss correlates strongly with downstream performance. This means you can select the best model checkpoint purely from the training loss, without needing to run expensive linear probing evaluations.

Scatter plot of training loss vs. downstream linear probe accuracy. Le-JEPA shows strong negative correlation (lower loss = higher accuracy). VICReg's correlation is much weaker.
